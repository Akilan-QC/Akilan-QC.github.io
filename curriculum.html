Curriculum Vitae

Postdoc 3 (June 2024)

I will be joining as a postdoc in ICGM UMR 5253, Montpelier. The primary job of the project will be designing an optimal implementation of Q-DFT in quantum devices and implementing them in BigDFT code.

Postdoc 2 (May 2023 - April 2024)

I was engaged in a research project titled "Rebirth of Aromatic Cope Rearrangements", under the guidance of Prof. Eric HENON in ICMR UMR 7312, URCA, Reims. This project involved collaboration with two distinct experimental groups. My work primarily revolved around utilizing quantum mechanical tools to investigate the impact of metal catalysts, various solvents, and functional groups on reaction mechanisms. Additionally, as a side endeavor, I was developing a promolecular density model that will be integrated into IGMplot (a versatile quantum mechanics tool capable of identifying and quantifying molecular interactions). Furthermore, I contributed to the implementation of Slater Type Orbitals (STO) within the IGMplot framework. Lastly, I employed machine learning techniques to predict interaction energies using descriptors derived from the promolecular density analysis.

Postdoc 1 (April 2021 - September 2022)

In my first postdoc, I was working on the heterogeneous reactions of Ozone and Sulphur dioxide on the surface of atmospheric aerosols using molecular dynamics simulations (MD) and QM/MM approach. The project was supervised by Prof. Celine TOUBIN and Dr. Denis DUFLOT in PhLAM UMR 8523, Universite de Lille. During my one and a half year of postdoctoral period in Lille, I also developed a graphical user interface with python (tkinter) and bash scripts, that connects two programmes for calculating reaction rates.

PhD in Physics (November 2016 - January 2021)

I completed my PhD in January 2021 under the guidance of Dr. R. Shankar in department of Physics, Bharathiar University, Coimbatore. My PhD thesis is entitled "The First Principle and Quantum Chemical Modelling of Carbon, Boron, Nitrogen and Silicon-Based Two-Dimensional Adsorbent Materials for Energy Harvesting Applications". In my PhD, I worked on Boron, Carbon, Nitrogen and Silicon-based Two-Dimensional materials as electrodes using Quantum chemical tools such as Gaussian, Quantum espresso and VASP. Further, I studied the defects of structures, adsorption and diffusion properties of Metal-ions, and molecules on the surface of electrodes. Furthermore, I also studied the electronic properties (using electron density studies such as AIM and ELF) of stacked Two-Dimensional materials and excited states using DFT and TD-DFT respectively. I had collaboration with people who are working on Atmospheric chemistry, DNA-ligand interaction, Drug delivery, DNA sensing, thermoelectric, supercapacitors and metal-ion batteries, and our work is published in reputed journals.
